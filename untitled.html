Robotics is applied in many fields. Physical therapy is one of these fields.  Also, the ankle sprain is a very common injury.
For physical therapy, special movements are necessary. Such movements include the active and passive range of motion, with special joint speed, acceleration, torque and force. Additionally, they must be gradual and carry a recuperation plan with long and tedious sessions.
The ankle biomechanical models are a powerful tool in the design of machine-human interaction. They include kinematics and dynamics of all soft tissue, bones, ligaments, surface joints, tendons, muscles, nerves, veins and skin tissue. Such models are complex, in the case of the ankle, is commonly accepted the two axes model. 
The parallel robots have good characteristics as lightweight, efficiency, speed, force and torque distribution and stiffness. A special kind of parallel robots with low mobility or restrictions are an interesting field. They can provide security by restraining only the movements reached by the ankle. 
Screw theory is a mathematical tool useful and easily visualized by geometry. Resembles the rigid body movement by displacement plus rotation about an axis
